Business Intelligence Framework for Cryptocurrency Prediction: A Comparative Study of LSTM, GRU, and Ensemble Baselines Using Big Data Analytics With Deep Learning Integration
Asep Surahman Sulaeman
Master of Informatics, Nusa Putra University, Sukabumi, Indonesia
Email: asep.surahman@nusaputra.ac.id
Abstract: The explosive volatility of cryptocurrency markets demands predictive models that seamlessly integrate real-time Big Data with strategic decision support systems. This study proposes a novel four-layer Business Intelligence (BI) Framework synthesizing Big Data Analytics with deep learning predictive engines. Utilizing a five-year high-frequency dataset comprising 43,788 hourly Bitcoin (BTC/USDT) records from the Binance API, we rigorously address the 5V dimensions of Big Data. Feature variety is mathematically captured through a comprehensive engineering process generating 15 technical indicators, evaluated via SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) to map specific algorithmic importances. The computational layer benchmarks advanced Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) and Gated Recurrent Units (GRU) sequential networks against an XGBoost ensemble baseline model. The deep architectures utilized hyperparameters optimized via automated Grid Search (two hidden layers, 50 nodes, 20% Dropout). Experimental Walk-Forward Validation reveals a critical architectural trade-off: The GRU model demonstrated undeniably superior margins regarding proximity precision (RMSE: 0.1058), definitively outperforming both LSTM (0.1145) and the XGBoost baseline (0.1524). However, the LSTM achieved significantly higher positive-directional trading accuracy (72.3%) compared to GRU (69.8%) and XGBoost (58.4%). Crucially, these deep learning parameters are subsequently routed into an algorithmic BI Presentation Layer mapping enterprise risk constraints via the Sharpe Ratio (LSTM: 1.45) and Value at Risk projections (VaR at 95%: -2.34%). Ultimately, this academic treatise culminates in a production-capable ecosystem proving that sequence-aware deep architectures successfully mitigate aggressive volatility while furnishing superior financial intelligence directly to institutional stakeholders.

Keywords—Business Intelligence, Cryptocurrency Prediction, Big Data Analytics, Deep Learning, XGBoost, LSTM, GRU, Financial Informatics, Bitcoin.

The digitization of global economic networks over the previous decade has rigidly established decentralized algorithmic assets—most notably Bitcoin (BTC)—as a dominant financial class. Characterized by unprecedented stochastics, continuous twenty-four-hour trading cycles, and extreme susceptibility to exogenous macroeconomic sentiment shocks, cryptocurrency environments formulate the pinnacle challenge for predictive computational modeling [1], [2], [3]. Traditional stoichiometric and econometric time-series models, heavily reliant upon rigid assumptions like stationarity (such as ARIMA or GARCH protocols), fall profoundly short when applied to High-Frequency Trading (HFT) matrices [4], [5]. These classical systems inherently fail to dynamically capture non-linear, temporal macro-dependencies completely obscured within high-frequency market noise [6].

Consequently, institutional market participants consistently migrate computational workloads toward deeper Neural Networks mathematically grounded within Big Data Analytics frameworks. The massive ingestion of transactional ledgers natively satisfies the foundational definitions of Big Data, thereby enabling recurrent deep learning models to systematically unravel convoluted trading relationships.

Literature Review and Theoretical Formulation

Deep learning forecasting within financial informatics possesses a well-documented genealogy. However, bridging continuous sequence architectures directly into distributed frameworks highlights continuous evolutionary academic strides.

A. Limitations of Traditional Modeling

Traditionally, financial price discovery operated heavily upon the Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH), mathematically assuming current valuations definitively reflect all public metadata. However, cryptocurrency operations famously exhibit long-memory effects proving significant market inefficiencies [7]. While autoregressive configurations (ARIMA) calculate near-term regression optimally across normalized fiat currencies, prior studies verify these mechanisms suffer immediate catastrophic gradient degradations when confronting massive variance spikes. Consequently, ensemble tree-based parameters (like XGBoost) evolved as reliable deterministic baselines across tabular financial constraints [8].

B. Evolution of Sequence Neural Topologies

Addressing the inherent vanishing gradient problem associated with simple RNN networks, Hochreiter and Schmidhuber [9] finalized the LSTM architecture, deploying independent gating variables strictly dictating memory cell preservation. To optimize processing density, Cho et al. [10] engineered the GRU variant, condensing algorithmic tensors by intentionally bypassing an independent internal storage state. While both topologies aggressively map high-frequency deviations [11], [12], recent literature evaluates their sequential strengths strictly juxtaposed against corporate Risk Strategy analytics integrating Business Intelligence reporting structures [13], [14].

C. Long Short-Term Memory Arrays

The core LSTM framework protects gradient propagation via designated algorithmic control gates handling temporal survival vectors natively governing Input (\(i_t\)) and Forget (\(f_t\)) restrictions:
